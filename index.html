Understanding Fragrance

  1. The Fragrance Pyramid Understanding how perfumes evolve over time is essential for appreciation. Fragrances unfold in three distinct stages, each revealing different characteristics.
    • Top Notes: The initial impression lasting 15 minutes to 2 hours
    • Heart Notes: The core personality emerging after 2-6 hours
    • Base Notes: The foundation lingering 6+ hours
    • Arabian perfumes often emphasize base notes for longevity
    • Lattafa specializes in complex base note compositions
  2. Concentration Levels The intensity and longevity of a fragrance depend on its concentration of perfume oils. Different concentrations suit different occasions and preferences.
    • Parfum (Extrait): 20-30% concentration, 8-24 hours longevity
    • Eau de Parfum (EDP): 15-20% concentration, 4-8 hours longevity
    • Eau de Toilette (EDT): 5-15% concentration, 2-4 hours longevity
    • Eau de Cologne: 2-4% concentration, 2-3 hours longevity
    • Lattafa primarily produces EDP for optimal value and performance
  3. Fragrance Families Perfumes are categorized into families based on their dominant characteristics. Understanding these helps identify your preferences.
    • Oriental: Warm, spicy, amber-rich with exotic resins
    • Woody: Centered on sandalwood, cedar, and oud
    • Floral: Featuring rose, jasmine, and orange blossom
    • Fresh: Citrus, aquatic, and green notes
    • Gourmand: Edible scents like vanilla, caramel, and spices
  4. Application Techniques Proper application enhances fragrance performance and longevity. Heat and pulse points are key to projection.
    • Apply to pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears
    • Don't rub wrists together—it breaks molecular structure
    • Spray on clothing for extended longevity
    • Moisturized skin holds fragrance longer
    • Layer with matching body products for intensity
  5. Storage and Preservation Protecting your investment requires proper storage. Light, heat, and air are perfume's enemies.
    • Store in cool, dark places away from sunlight
    • Keep bottles in original boxes for extra protection
    • Avoid bathroom storage due to humidity fluctuations
    • Ensure caps are tightly sealed to prevent oxidation
    • Most perfumes last 3-5 years when stored properly

Why Arabian Perfumes?

Arabian perfumery represents one of the oldest and most sophisticated fragrance traditions in the world. Dating back thousands of years, this art form has perfected the use of oud, amber, musk, and exotic spices to create scents that are both powerful and refined. Unlike Western perfumery, which often emphasizes freshness and subtlety, Arabian fragrances celebrate intensity, complexity, and longevity.

Lattafa Perfumes embodies this tradition while embracing modern innovation. Based in Dubai, the brand has mastered the art of creating accessible luxury—offering complex, high-quality compositions at prices that democratize niche perfumery. Their fragrances often feature oud in various forms, from the medicinal intensity of pure oud to the smoother, more wearable interpretations blended with vanilla, rose, or spices.

The appeal of Arabian perfumes extends beyond the Middle East. Globally, fragrance enthusiasts are discovering the unique character of these scents—their ability to project confidence, evoke mystery, and leave memorable impressions. Whether you prefer the smoky depth of Oud For Glory or the sweet warmth of Khamrah, Arabian perfumes offer a distinctive alternative to mainstream Western fragrances.
