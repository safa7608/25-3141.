Fragrance Guide

  1. The Fragrance Pyramid Understanding how perfumes evolve over time is essential for appreciation.
    • Top Notes: Initial impression lasting 15 minutes to 2 hours
    • Heart Notes: Core personality emerging after 2-6 hours
    • Base Notes: Foundation lingering 6+ hours
    • Arabian perfumes emphasize base notes for longevity
    • Lattafa specializes in complex base compositions
  2. Concentration Levels The intensity depends on perfume oil concentration.
    • Parfum (Extrait): 20-30% concentration, 8-24 hours
    • Eau de Parfum (EDP): 15-20% concentration, 4-8 hours
    • Eau de Toilette (EDT): 5-15% concentration, 2-4 hours
    • Eau de Cologne: 2-4% concentration, 2-3 hours
    • Lattafa primarily produces EDP for optimal value
  3. Fragrance Families Categories based on dominant characteristics.
    • Oriental: Warm, spicy, amber-rich with exotic resins
    • Woody: Centered on sandalwood, cedar, and oud
    • Floral: Featuring rose, jasmine, orange blossom
    • Fresh: Citrus, aquatic, and green notes
    • Gourmand: Edible scents like vanilla and caramel
  4. Application Techniques Proper application enhances performance.
    • Apply to pulse points: wrists, neck, behind ears
    • Don't rub wrists together—breaks molecular structure
    • Spray on clothing for extended longevity
    • Moisturized skin holds fragrance longer
    • Layer with matching products for intensity
  5. Storage and Preservation Protecting your investment requires proper care.
    • Store in cool, dark places away from sunlight
    • Keep bottles in original boxes for protection
    • Avoid bathroom storage due to humidity
    • Ensure caps are tightly sealed
    • Most perfumes last 3-5 years when stored properly
